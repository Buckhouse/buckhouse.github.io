Questions No One Cares to Ask Me

How do you track wild mustangs?

Across a million and a half acres of BLM land in the deserts of Eastern and Southern Oregon, I spent a week tracking wild mustangs with my father. Eventually we found a running bachelor who had lost a fight for dominance. Based on his path combined with likely sources of water, we found hidden in the folds of a hillside the rest of the pack. For a few brief moments I managed to film them up close. The results of that trip became the source material for a gallery show of paintings in LA.

Who is the World's Greatest Bikini Designer?

I wrote a satirical novel about the world’s greatest bikini designer—exploring the existential struggles of four triangles held together with a piece of string. I cover some of the same territory as other books on art and poetics, but sneak it in with a laugh. The book was a part of a solo exhibition at the Berkeley Museum of Art. The NY Times wrote a flattering review of it where they graciously played along with just enough of a wink.

How do you get people to partocipate in Friends and Strangers?

I have an on-going portrait series called Friends and Strangers where I paint watercolor portraits of interesting people. The best approach is to walk up and ask.

How do you write a joke?

When writing jokes for DreamWorks, it’s best to do it in groups of three or more writers—example: I wrote the least funny 1/3 of the Mission-Impossible-Pinocchio-in-a-thong joke. Most of my jokes for DreamWorks were sight gags involving a prop, such as a medieval mechanical bull or an obscure cultural reference, such as a visual nod to a moment from Un Chien Andalou, Last Year at Marienband, or Corsaire. When writing jokes for Investors, CEOs or tech executives, my main adivice is this: keep the words short.
